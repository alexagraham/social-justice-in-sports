The Fight Against Racism in Professional Sports

The Goal of This Site

The goal of this site is to show how professional atheltes are using their image and popularity to support social justice reform. Amid a global pandemic, Racial injustice is at the forefront in the fight for a better America. Starting with the death of George Floyd, the tragic and unjust treatment of black people became just as large a fight as the pandemic itself. With racial tensions heightened and America hopefully turning a corner for the better, the fight only gets more attention and more support when some of the top athletes in the world show their support for the cause and that they are right there in it too.

Naomi Osaka, the 2020 U.S. Open Champion, brought seven different masks to New York. Each with a name printed on it of a black individual who lost their life unjustly. This was one of the biggest talks of the tournament and the fact that she got to play, and win, seven matches meant that the Black Lives Matter movement was supported seven times over, very publicly.

Lebron James, arguably the greatest basketabll player of all time, wore different shirts through out his warm-ups during the NBA season. Each shirt had a different quote in support of the Black Lives Matter movement. He was one of the leading forces in the NBA to confront social unjust. Him, along with some teammates, knelt to the National Anthem while wearing Black Lives Matter shirts, to show their support for the lives unjustly lost.

NFL players are not new to this movement, given that Colin Kaepernick has played a big role in the Black Lives Matter movement for years now. Except this time, there seemed to be a greater league wide support, as racial injustice is right in front of us. Many players across the league wore decals on their helmets with words of support and unity. Some of these helmets consisted of names of the lives lost unjustly. Others were words like "unity", or "Black Lives Matter".

To read more about a certain athlete or league in their support for Black Lives Matter, please click the card with their photo on it and you will be directed to the article. You can always return home by pressing the logo in the top left corner.
